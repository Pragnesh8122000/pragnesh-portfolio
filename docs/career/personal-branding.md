# 🌐 Personal Branding — Get Found, Get Hired

> **The best job search is the one where recruiters come to you.**
> **Build in public. Share what you learn. Network relentlessly.**

---

## 🎯 Why this matters (the math)

- **~50% of jobs at 20+ LPA are filled via referral.** Not via Naukri.
- **~30% come via inbound** (recruiter reaches out to you).
- **~20% come via cold applying.** (This is the lowest-leverage channel.)

**Personal branding flips the math:** you become the person recruiters chase, hiring managers DMs, and peers refer.

### Posting strategy (1x/week, non-negotiable)

**The 5 post types that work:**

**1. Project launch (best engagement)**
```
🚀 I just shipped [Project Name].

Problem: [1 sentence]
Solution: [1 sentence]
Stack: [3-4 technologies]
Demo: [link to live demo or video]

What I learned:
- [Insight 1]
- [Insight 2]
- [Insight 3]

Code: [GitHub link]

#AI #LLM #MachineLearning
```

**2. TIL (Today I Learned)**
```
TIL: [concise insight]

I was building [X] and discovered [Y].

Before: I thought [old belief]
After: I learned [new understanding]

Example:
[code snippet or screenshot]

Wrote a longer breakdown: [blog link if you have one]

#AI #LLM
```

**3. Comparison / Review**
```
I tried [A] vs [B] for [task]. Here's what I found.

Setup: [brief]
Results: [numbers or screenshots]

Winner: [X] for [use case]
But [Y] is better for [other use case]

Code: [link]
```

**4. Hot take**
```
Unpopular opinion: [controversial but defensible AI take]

Here's why: [2-3 sentences of reasoning]

What do you think? 👇

#AI
```

**5. Tutorial / How-to (long-form)**
```
How to [achieve X] in 2026 (step-by-step):

1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
...

Tools used: [list]
Code: [link]

Save this for later 🔖
```

### Engagement tactics (30 min/day)

- **Comment on 5-10 posts/day** from senior AI folks. Add real value, not "great post!"
- **DM 2-3 new people/week** — senior engineers, recruiters, hiring managers
- **Congratulate people** on work anniversaries, new jobs, posts
- **Reply to comments on your posts** within 1 hour (LinkedIn algorithm loves this)

### Growth hacks

- **Cross-post from Twitter/X** to LinkedIn (and vice versa)
- **Use 3-5 hashtags** per post (not 30)
- **Post Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM** (peak engagement for Indian audience)
- **Tag people/companies** when relevant (they often like or share)
- **Use carousel posts** (PDFs) — LinkedIn algorithm loves them, 2x reach

## 🐦 Twitter/X — Optional but powerful

Many AI folks are on Twitter. Pros: faster feedback, more technical crowd, easier to grow. Cons: more noise, time-sink.

**If you go for it:**
- Post 3-5x/week
- Same content types as LinkedIn
- Engage with AI Twitter (Hugging Face, LangChain, LlamaIndex, etc.)
- Use threads for tutorials

**If not:** focus 100% on LinkedIn. Quality > quantity of platforms.

## ✍️ Writing — The ultimate differentiator

**Writing proves you understand.** A blog post or technical thread is worth 10 GitHub repos in terms of credibility.

**Start a blog:**
- [Hashnode](https://hashnode.com/) — best for devs, free, custom domain
- [Dev.to](https://dev.to/) — easy, big audience
- Personal blog on Next.js (uses your existing skills)

**What to write about:**
- "How I built [project]" — case study
- "Comparing [tool A] vs [tool B]" — tutorial
- "RAG evaluation: what I learned" — depth
- "5 things I wish I knew before fine-tuning" — opinion

**Aim for 1 post/month.** That's 12 in a year. You'll be a known voice.

## 🤝 Networking — The multiplier

### Find your 20 "connectors"

These are 20 people you should know personally by the end of 12 months:
- 5 senior AI engineers at target companies
- 5 AI engineering recruiters
- 5 AI startup founders or PMs
- 5 AI community members (Discord, meetup, etc.)

**How to find them:**
- Search LinkedIn for "AI Engineer" at companies you want to work for
- Look at who's posting good AI content (these are usually responsive)
- Ask for intros from your current network

**How to reach out (template):**
> Hi [Name], I've been following your work on [topic] — loved your [post/talk/repo]. I'm transitioning from web dev to AI engineering and your journey is inspiring. Would you be open to a 15-min chat sometime? No pitch, just want to learn. Thanks!

**Once you have 1-2 calls:**
- Be curious, not pitching
- Send a thank-you note after
- Stay in touch (occasional LinkedIn like, comment)
- Refer jobs to them (yes, even when YOU need one — give first)

## 🎤 Speaking & Community

**High leverage, low frequency:**
- Speak at 1-2 meetups in your city (find them on Meetup.com, Luma)
- Volunteer to talk at a local dev community
- Apply to speak at AI conferences (PyConf, IndiaFOSS, ODSC)

**Why it works:** Hiring managers scan speaker lists. Speaking = credibility on steroids.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

1. ❌ **Polishing profile, never posting.** Done > perfect.
2. ❌ **Posting and ghosting.** Engagement (replying to comments) is what compounds.
3. ❌ **"Building in public" without building.** Content ≠ code. Ship first.
4. ❌ **Cold-DMing "can you refer me?"** Awkward. Build relationship first.
5. ❌ **Negativity on LinkedIn.** Even justified. Keep it professional.
6. ❌ **Trying to be everywhere.** LinkedIn + GitHub is enough. Skip Twitter if no time.
