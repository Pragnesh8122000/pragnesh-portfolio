## 🧠 The meta-skills nobody teaches

These separate 20 LPA from 50 LPA:

- **Communication** — write clearly, explain technical to non-technical, present well.
- **System design for AI** — caching, queues, cost, observability, evaluation loops.
- **Cost awareness** — you know how to cut LLM bills by 80% with caching, batching, smaller models.
- **Evals** — you can measure if your AI system is actually good, not just "feels good."
- **Prompt engineering as engineering** — versioned, tested, A/B'd, not vibes.

## ⚠️ Common traps to avoid

1. ❌ **Tutorial hell** — watching 100 hours of YouTube, building nothing. STOP after 30% of any course. Start building.
2. ❌ **Math perfectionism** — you don't need to derive backprop by hand. You need to understand it intuitively. Skip the proofs.
3. ❌ **PhD envy** — you don't need a PhD. You need 5 shipped projects and a strong LinkedIn.
4. ❌ **Comparing to ML researchers** — they get paid less than AI Engineers right now. Weird but true.
5. ❌ **Cold applying on Naukri** — 95% of 20+ LPA jobs come via referral. Network.
